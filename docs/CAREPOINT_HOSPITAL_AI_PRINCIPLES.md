# CarePoint Hospital AI Principles Statement

## Purpose and Scope

In alignment with CarePoint Hospital's values of patient safety and quality of care, equity and non-discrimination, privacy and dignity, and clinical excellence grounded in evidence-based care, we commit to the ethical and responsible deployment, operational use, and post-deployment monitoring of AI systems.

These principles apply across CarePoint Hospital service lines, including inpatient, outpatient, surgery, and emergency care, and to all AI-enabled workflows that may influence operational decisions, clinical support, patient communication, or resource utilization.

## Core Principles

### 1) System Trustworthiness

Every AI system deployed or used by CarePoint Hospital shall be designed, implemented, and monitored to achieve appropriate trustworthiness outcomes for its specific clinical or operational context, with documented trade-offs where needed.

a) **Validity and Reliability**  
AI systems shall consistently perform within defined acceptance thresholds under expected real-world conditions and patient populations.

b) **Safety**  
AI systems shall not be used in ways that create unacceptable risk to patient safety, staff safety, or continuity of care.

c) **Security and Resilience**  
AI systems and associated data flows shall maintain confidentiality, integrity, and availability, including resilience to misuse, cyber threats, model degradation, and operational disruption.

d) **Accountability and Transparency**  
Roles, decision rights, and escalation paths for each AI system shall be clearly assigned, and meaningful information about system purpose, limits, and performance shall be communicated to relevant stakeholders.

e) **Explainability and Interpretability**  
AI-assisted outputs used in care or operations shall be accompanied, where technically feasible, by interpretable rationale, confidence context, and usage constraints to support informed human judgment.

f) **Privacy-Enhanced Design**  
AI systems shall protect patient and workforce privacy through appropriate safeguards, minimal necessary data use, secure handling of health information, and compliance with applicable legal and regulatory requirements.

g) **Fairness with Harmful Bias Managed**  
AI systems shall be assessed and monitored for harmful bias, with mitigation actions implemented when inequitable performance or outcomes are detected across relevant patient groups.

### 2) Human Oversight and Accountability

CarePoint Hospital affirms that AI supports, but does not replace, professional judgment and duty of care. Clinical decisions with material patient impact remain under accountable human oversight by appropriately qualified personnel. Governance mechanisms shall include:

- defined human review checkpoints for high-impact use cases;
- clear incident reporting and escalation procedures;
- periodic review of model behavior, errors, and near-misses;
- independent assurance activities where appropriate (for example audits or external assessments).

### 3) Beneficence, Equity, and Ethics

CarePoint Hospital shall use AI to improve patient outcomes, operational efficiency, and responsible resource utilization while preserving human dignity and promoting equitable access to quality care. AI use shall be rejected, redesigned, or restricted when ethical risk is disproportionate to expected benefit.

### 4) Continual Learning and Improvement

Given evolving technology, clinical evidence, and regulation, CarePoint Hospital commits to continuous learning in AI governance and operations. This includes ongoing monitoring, post-deployment validation, stakeholder feedback loops, workforce capability building, and periodic policy updates to maintain responsible AI practice over time.

## Implementation Commitments

To operationalize these principles, CarePoint Hospital will maintain documented processes for:

- AI risk and impact assessment before and during use;
- performance, safety, and fairness monitoring after deployment;
- change management and revalidation when systems or contexts materially change;
- incident response, corrective action, and organizational learning.

## Compliance and Traceability Note

This statement is intended to align with the intent of ISO/IEC 42001 AI governance requirements for AI policy, risk and impact management, operational control, monitoring, and continual improvement, and with the trustworthiness characteristics described in the NIST AI RMF.
