# LibertyMD Clinical Review Checklist

## Scenario Review

- [ ] Patient age, sex, comorbidities, medicines, allergies, and pregnancy context are clinically plausible.
- [ ] Expected emergency action is correct for the exact wording, including negations and atypical presentations.
- [ ] Expected care setting is appropriate for the intended launch country.
- [ ] Missing or contradictory information is represented as unknown rather than affirmative evidence.
- [ ] The expected differential and supporting/conflicting evidence are clinically defensible.
- [ ] Red flags and safety-net language are complete and understandable.
- [ ] The scenario does not encode a demographic stereotype or unsupported assumption.
- [ ] Reviewer name, credentials, review date, decision, and notes are recorded.

## Release Review

- [ ] Emergency sensitivity and specificity targets have named clinical approval.
- [ ] The reviewed dataset represents common, rare, atypical, and negated presentations.
- [ ] Pediatric, pregnancy, older-adult, medication, mental-health, and multimorbidity scope is explicit.
- [ ] Confidence and evidence thresholds match the approved clinical policy.
- [ ] Patient-facing limitations and escalation language match the product's actual capabilities.
- [ ] Model, prompt, workflow, schema, and policy versions match the registry.
- [ ] All automated contracts, database tests, and the reviewed scenario suite pass.
- [ ] Rollback has been rehearsed and has an accountable owner.

Approval of an engineering fixture is not approval of LibertyMD as a medical device, clinical service, or replacement for professional care.
